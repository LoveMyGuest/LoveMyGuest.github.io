What will you experience?

It is not often possible to taste wines coming from vineyards with an average life between 40 and 50 years of life, let alone have the chance to see some vineyards with up to 80 years of age. Matteo Correggia winery has about 20 selected hectares, where the vines of Roero are growing: Nebbiolo, Barbera, Arneis and Brachetto.

During the visit, Matteo, Ornella or Brigitta will lead you in a tour inside their winery, narrating you the hystory of their family, the secrets of their land and, of course, the different expressions of Roero's vines.

During the visit, you will have the opportunity to feel the passion for winemaking, to meet Luca, Davide, Pietro, or any other important person that contributes everyday to make Matteo Correggia one the best winemaker among italian ones.

The wine tasting takes place in a beautiful cellar under the hill, in contact with the millenary marls rich in fossils, where humidity and quiet reign supreme. Woods and stems are selected according to the wines they are destined to host, the result of twenty years of practice.The quietness, the optimal environment and the silence of the cellar allow you to enjoy the wines during the tasting.

This is a tour suitable for those who have an in-depth knowledge of the sector and want to discover the wines of Roero, but also to newbies who will learn a lot thanks to the Correggia family and their knowledge of Roero wines and the technical, olfactory and sensorial characteristics of each wine.

Here you can find the available experience:

  • "A taste of Roero Hills": guided visit to the cellar and 4 wines tasting including Roero Arneis, Barbera d'Alba DOC, Roero DOCG, Roero "La Val dei Preti" DOCG . (1 hour ca- 20€ per person)
  • "The Crus of Roero": guided visit to the cellar and tasting of 3 Superior and Reserva Roero wines, including Barbera Superiore "Marun" DOC, Roero "La Val dei Preti" DOCG, Roero Riserva "Ròche d'Ampsej . (1 hour ca- 25€ per person)
  • "The Matteo Correggia inheritance": a complete experience including a guided visit to the cellar and + tasting of all 10 wines obtained from over 20 hectares of wineyards. (1 hour and a half - 35€ per person) 

At the end of the visit you will have the opportunity to purchase the wine at a competitive price also in relation to many online sites, thanks also to the advices of an oenologist expert and his collaborators

About "Matteo Correggia"


Matteo Correggia was evolutionary, a pioneer, who was the first to look beyond the hills of Roero and focus on the bigger picture.

He started the business in 1985, when he inherited the lands and farm from his father, Giovanni Battista. 

Even if in those years Roero was not as popular as today and wines produced were mainly consumed by family and friends, Matteo had the courage to against the tide and focuses on sustainable viticulture and quality, for creating a new brand connected to Roero and exporting it around the world.

His revolutionary work was soon noticed by Roberto Voerzio and Elio Altare, who started giving him advice, following his progress: in a short time, Matteo joined the Barolo Boys, becoming the only non-Barolo wine producer in the group. 
Along the year, he contributed to enhance the Roero terroir by leveraging on distictive traits of the-old sandy soil of Roero.

Unfortunately, in 2001, Matteo dies whilst working in his beloved vineyards. Fate got a big trick to the Correggia family, bringing with him one of the raising star of the priod. 

But Correggia's family remained strong and, folowing the track laid by Matteo, they continued the family-business, always under the banner of innovation, love and respect for Roero.
Today, the whole wine orchestra is run by Ornella, Matteo's wife, with the great support of theirs sons, Giovanni and Brigitta, who, day by day, contribute to lead thos faboulous winery towards the future.

Matteo Correggia is not only a winemaking company, is a more holistic outline where the passion for wine and terroir have met the love for the tradition, for friends and other form of art.
